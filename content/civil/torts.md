## Introduction

A **Tort** is a "civil wrong" that causes someone else to suffer loss or harm. Unlike criminal law where the state punishes the offender, tort law allows the injured person (the plaintiff) to sue the wrongdoer (the defendant) for financial compensation.

The word "tort" comes from French meaning "wrong."

## Types of Torts

There are three main categories of torts based on the intent of the person who caused the harm:

### 1. Intentional Torts
These happen when someone *purposely* acts in a way that harms another person.
*   **Examples:** Assault, battery, trespassing on someone's land, or defamation (saying false things to ruin a reputation).

### 2. Negligence (The most common tort)
These happen when someone acts carelessly or fails to take reasonable precautions, resulting in harm.
*   **Examples:** Car accidents, slip-and-fall injuries in a supermarket, medical malpractice.

### 3. Strict Liability
These apply in situations where someone is held responsible for harm, regardless of whether they intended to cause it or were careless. It applies to inherently dangerous activities.
*   **Examples:** Owning a wild or dangerous animal, or manufacturing a defective product (like a phone battery that unexpectedly explodes).

## Real-Life Example: Negligence

> **Scenario:**
> You are texting while driving and accidentally rear-end the car in front of you. 

You didn't *intend* to hurt the other driver (so it's not an intentional tort). However, as a driver, you have a "duty of care" to pay attention to the road. By texting, you breached that duty. Because your carelessness caused damage to the other car and whiplash to the driver, you committed the tort of **Negligence**. 
The other driver can sue you (or your insurance) in civil court for the cost of car repairs and medical bills.

## Key Takeaways

*   A tort is a civil wrong that causes injury or loss.
*   Tort law aims to "make the victim whole again" through financial compensation.
*   **Negligence** is the failure to take proper care, resulting in damage or injury to another.
