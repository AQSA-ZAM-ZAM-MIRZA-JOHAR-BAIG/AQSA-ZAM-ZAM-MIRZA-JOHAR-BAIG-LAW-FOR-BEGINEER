## Introduction

Property law governs the various forms of ownership in real property (land) and personal property. For most people, buying a house is the single largest financial transaction of their lives, making property law incredibly important.

## Types of Property

In law, property is generally divided into two main categories:

1.  **Real Property (Real Estate):** Land and anything permanently attached to it, like a house, a building, or even trees growing on the land.
2.  **Personal Property:** Everything else that is movable. This includes your car, your laptop, furniture, and money. Personal property can also be intangible, like a copyright or shares in a company.

## Buying Real Estate: The Basics

When you buy a house or land, you aren't just handing over money; you are legally transferring the "Title" of the property.

*   **The Title:** Think of the title as the legal concept of ownership. Having the title means you have the right to use, sell, or modify the property.
*   **The Deed:** The deed is the physical legal document that transfers the title from the seller to the buyer. You must officially register this deed with the local government to prove you are the new owner.

## What is a Mortgage?

Most people cannot pay for a house in full with cash. They take out a loan called a **mortgage**.

A mortgage is a specific type of loan where the real estate itself serves as collateral. This means if you fail to repay the loan, the bank has the legal right to take ownership of the house and sell it to get their money back. This process is called **foreclosure**.

## Let's Talk About Renting (Landlord/Tenant Law)

When you rent an apartment, you don't own the property, but you sign a **Lease Agreement**. This is a contract that gives you the right to *exclusive possession* of the property for a set amount of time.
*   **Tenant Rights:** You have the right to a safe, habitable living space. The landlord cannot barge in unannounced; you have a right to privacy.
*   **Landlord Rights:** The landlord has the right to receive rent on time and expect that you won't purposefully destroy the property.

## Key Takeaways
*   **Real Property** is land and buildings.
*   A **Deed** is the document that transfers ownership.
*   A **Mortgage** uses your house as collateral for a loan.
*   Renting is governed by specific **Landlord-Tenant contracts** that protect both parties.
