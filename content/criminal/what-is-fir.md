## Introduction

An **F.I.R.** stands for **First Information Report**. Whenever a crime occurs, the very first step in starting a police investigation is filing an FIR. As the name suggests, it is the *first information* given to the police regarding a crime.

## When Should an FIR Be Filed?

An FIR is only filed for **cognizable offenses**. A cognizable offense is a serious crime (like theft, murder, kidnapping, or severe assault) where the police have the authority to make an arrest without needing a warrant from a judge. 

For minor offenses (non-cognizable offenses), like public nuisance or a minor argument, the police usually require a magistrate's permission to investigate.

## How to File an FIR

Filing an FIR is a right. Here is how it generally works:

1. **Go to the Police Station:** Visit the station nearest to where the crime occurred.
2. **Report the Incident:** You can report it verbally or in writing. If verbal, the officer must write it down.
3. **Review the Report:** **Always** read what the officer has written before signing. If you cannot read, they must read it back to you.
4. **Sign the FIR:** Your signature makes the document official.
5. **Get a Copy:** You have a legal right to receive a copy of your FIR completely **free of charge**.

## Real-Life Example

> **Scenario:**
> You wake up to find your car has been stolen from your driveway. 

You must immediately go to the nearest police station. You tell the officer what happened—the make, model, and registration number of the car, and what time you noticed it missing. The officer writes this down, you sign it, and you are handed a copy. This document is the FIR. Only after this document is created can the police officially begin trying to find your car.

## Key Takeaways

* FIR stands for First Information Report.
* It is the starting point of any criminal investigation for serious crimes.
* You have a legal right to a free copy of your FIR.
* Always read the FIR before signing it!
